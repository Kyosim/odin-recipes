Soy Marinated Egg

Description

Delicious boiled eggs marinated in a savoury soy sauce broth

This is a common dish enjoyed by Koreans for its simplicity and salty flavor! You can have the eggs on their own with a bowl of rice, or combine them with some other side dishes to your liking. Versatility is a real pro for this dish! As long as you have the patience to wait for the eggs to really soak in the soy sauce, you'll have a great meal ready for you anytime you're hungry.

Ingredients

  • Soy sauce
  • Eggs (however many you like)
  • Vinegar
  • Ginger and Garlic (optional)
  1. Bring a pot of water to a roiling boil. Add your eggs.
  2. Boil for about 7 minutes. You can adjust the time, if you prefer your egg yolk harder or softer.
  3. While the eggs are boiling, you may finely mince some ginger and garlic, if you'd like to add them to your marinade.
  4. Prepare a container large enough for your eggs. Fill about halfway with soy sauce, and a tablespoon or so of vinegar.
  5. Add the ginger and garlic, if you chose to use them.
  6. Finally, once the eggs are done, lightly wash in cold water and peel the shells.
  7. Add the eggs to the marinade and refrigerate. Let sit for at least 12 hours.
